## Layer 2: Synthesized operating profile

### Positioning
- One-line positioning: The only ad tool that reverse-engineers WHY competitor ads win, then builds creatives engineered to the same psychology.
- Key alternative it is beating: Manual swipe files + ad libraries (AdSpy, Meta Ad Library) + creative agencies that guess
- Buyer job: Win on paid social faster, with less creative waste

### Audience model
- Primary buyer: Head of Growth or Creative Strategist at a DTC/performance brand, or Founder running their own paid social
- Awareness stage: Problem-aware (knows competitor ads are winning, doesn't know the psychological WHY, hasn't seen this tool)
- Sophistication: High — these are experienced media buyers / growth operators who've tried other tools and been disappointed
- Pain priorities:
  1. Time wasted on inspiration loops that don't convert
  2. Surface-level ad copying that misses the psychological driver
  3. Creative team bandwidth / cost constraints
  4. Competitor advantage that feels unexplainable
- Desire priorities:
  1. A systematic edge over competitors
  2. Speed: ready-to-run creatives, not 10-step briefing processes
  3. Understanding: knowing WHY something works, not just copying it
  4. Scale: bulk generation for testing at volume

### Voice system
| Dimension | Position | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| formal ↔ casual | casual-direct | "YOUR COMPETITORS' ADS ARE WINNING" — blunt, no corporate hedge |
| reserved ↔ bold | very bold | All-caps headlines, "steal" verb, confrontational tone |
| technical ↔ accessible | balanced | "psychological elements" but also "ready-to-run creative" |
| practical ↔ aspirational | practical | Output is concrete: creatives you can run, not a vision |

### Language bank
- Words they use: steal, decode, psychology, high-performing, ready-to-run, competitors, winning, generate, creatives, brand
- Category terms expected by buyers: ad creative, performance creative, creative strategy, paid social, A/B testing, bulk variants, ad library
- Differentiator phrases: "steal the psychology" / "decode the psychology" / "not just what they're running — why it's working"
- Words and tones to avoid: "leverage," "elevate," "seamless experience," passive voice, airy aspiration without concrete output

  - Text: #1a1a1a on light surfaces, #f5f0e8 on dark
  - Surface dark: #111111 for card/UI dark panels

- Spacing density: Generous on page, dense on feature grid (bento style)
- Trust-critical conventions: Testimonial cards with name+role, pricing grid with plan names, dark UI for SaaS credibility
